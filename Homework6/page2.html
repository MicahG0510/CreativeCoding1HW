The mad king

Heavy lies the crown

His subjects at his feet

He sits upon the throne

The one he’ll never reach

He wishes for approval

He needs to be the best

The strongest and the bravest

Such burdens on his chest

The world will knock him down

The crown will bid him up

He gives all unto his people

Yet they will give him naught

The pain, the fear, the misery

He takes them on so none will see

His silent cries and hearts unrest

He packs inside his mangled chest

There’s light inside his dying eyes

The brightness of his smile shines

    He gives his all yet all he wants

    Is nothing but a single thanks

Great pride he took within his toils

Yet still his name the people soiled

His mind is taxed his soul grows tired

Something clicks, his mind expires

The death of pride is the death of reason

The king no longer stands their treason

Yet still they laugh, they mock, they holler

Their souls are black, their hearts are squalor

The king lets off a chilling cackle

He steps down from his throne unshackled

Their folly grasped a moment late

A journey to the hells await

They took for granted all he gave

Which lead them to an early grave

His city burns, the world as well

A throne is all that’s left to quell

A throne of ash he sits upon

The weight upon his head is gone
